# Scopeglass Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Scopeglass a respectful,
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression,
experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, or sexual and romantic identity or orientation.

We will act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that supports the community include:

- showing empathy and respect for different viewpoints and experiences;
- giving and receiving specific, constructive technical feedback;
- focusing criticism on ideas, code, and observable behavior rather than
  people;
- acknowledging mistakes, apologizing, and repairing harm where possible;
- respecting privacy, attribution, and coordinated vulnerability disclosure;
- prioritizing the health of the project and community over individual status.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, threats, or personal attacks;
- public or private harassment, stalking, or sustained disruption;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- discriminatory jokes, slurs, exclusion, or deliberate misgendering;
- pressuring someone to disclose identity, credentials, or personal context;
- retaliating against a person who reports a concern in good faith;
- other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

Technical disagreement is welcome. Contempt, intimidation, and harassment are
not.

## Scope

This Code applies in project spaces and whenever someone officially represents
Scopeglass. Project spaces include issues, discussions, reviews, commits,
release channels, community chat, events, and private communication connected
to project work.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They
may edit, reject, or remove comments, commits, code, documentation, issues, and
other contributions that violate this Code, and will communicate moderation
reasons when appropriate and safe.

Maintainers must handle reports as privately as practical, avoid conflicts of
interest, and recuse themselves when they are directly involved in a report.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately through the maintainer contact mechanism
provided by the repository hosting platform. Do not open a public issue that
identifies affected people or contains private evidence. If no suitable private
maintainer channel is available—or if a maintainer is involved—use the hosting
platform's abuse or safety reporting channel.

Include the relevant location, approximate time, people involved, description,
and any evidence you can share safely. Reports made in good faith will be
reviewed promptly and fairly. Information will be shared only as needed to
investigate, protect participants, and apply an outcome.

Security vulnerabilities follow the separate private process in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Enforcement guidelines

The response will be proportionate to context, impact, and pattern of behavior:

1. **Correction:** A private explanation and request to correct minor or
   apparently unintentional behavior.
2. **Warning:** A formal warning with clear expectations and consequences for
   continued behavior.
3. **Temporary restriction:** Time-limited removal from project interaction or
   representation when behavior is serious or repeated.
4. **Permanent restriction:** Permanent removal for sustained violations,
   harassment, threats, retaliation, or conduct that makes safe participation
   impossible.

An action may begin at any level when severity warrants it. Knowingly false or
malicious reports may themselves violate this Code; an unsubstantiated
good-faith report does not.

## Appeals

A person subject to an enforcement action may request reconsideration through
the same private channel. The request should identify the decision, relevant
context, and the outcome sought. When possible, a maintainer who did not make
the original decision will review the appeal.
